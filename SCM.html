8. Intro to Inventory Management
Use the calculator tools above to compute results. This section lists formulas only.

Chapter 8 Formulas

Inventory Turns = Flow Rate / Inventory

Inventory Turns = COGS / Average Inventory

Days of Supply = Inventory / Flow Rate

Days of Supply = 365 / Turns

Turns = 365 / Days of Supply

Annual Holding Cost = Unit Cost × Holding Cost Rate

Variables and Key Reminders

Symbol / Term Meaning
Flow Rate (R) Units flowing through the system per unit time
COGS Cost of Goods Sold
Average Inventory (I) Average inventory level in the system
Turns How many times inventory flows through the system in a year
Days of Supply How long inventory stays in the system
Unit Cost Purchase or production cost per unit
Holding Cost Rate Annual holding cost as a percentage of unit cost
When inventory is measured in cost dollars, use COGS (not revenue) as the flow rate when calculating inventory turns or days of supply.
